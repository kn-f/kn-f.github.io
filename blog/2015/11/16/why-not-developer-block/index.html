Why Not - Developer Block

2015-11-16 3:04 pm

Always the same old story: now I am able to program now not…

What makes the difference? And is the problem just for programming?

You read a lot of stuff around about on self help, on what you should do to live better and so on. At first I looked at these for inspiration, to see if I could find a help for me, then I started realizing that all these methods work, but for those who wrote them.

One of the thing I learned is to listen to myself, to my needs. Sometimes my body or my brain was giving me hints: tiredness, apathy, aggressiveness, muscular pains…If you’re healthy you should not experience these, at least not for a sustained amount of time. Small side note: I’m being over generic on these terms because it all really depends on you, if you’re usually grumpy even an excess of happyness could indicate that something’s wrong.

I’m sure you already experienced this. What did you do? Call it a day? Run and do something to distract you? No, there’s no one fits all solution, only you can listen to what your body tells you and only you will be the one able to reply properly.

The more I’m aware soemthing’s not right with me, the easier it is to manage my developer’s block. Now I just want to enjoy myself, I don’t have deadlines, huge expectations or problems of milions. It’s my hobby and when it starts to be frustrating, I stop, maybe I’ll come back later when I’ll have a freesher mind.
